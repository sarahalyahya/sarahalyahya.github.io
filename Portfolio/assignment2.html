a love letter.

A Web Comic
link documentation

This project idea came up in a group conversation where we were sharing cultural influences from back home. a group member had mentioned a tradition he has witnessed in Japanese high schools, were individuals “confess” their feelings to their crush, and wait for a response — either a yes or a no. In our story, however, it takes her a little while to say “yes,” but our protagonist was still there, patiently waiting.

I was mainly responsible for the visuals in this assignment, specifically the text boxes and collages. Again, you can notice my DIY approach. I initially suggested using collages as I thought it would be a fast option that gives an (almost) realistic feel, but not too realistic. While it certainly was not fast, it felt like a perfect fit for a story that can be very relatable for some. It was also a unique approach to experiment with web comic visuals! The final page with the polaroids was inspired from my first project which features a polaroid-like visual with scribbles.

Working on this project and trying to find a way to balance between words and visuals was a bit of a challenge, but having people from different backgrounds and with different creative skills made the process much easier and more enjoyable!
